Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Ohlone Sky, the Bay Area’s next iconic transit system.

What is Ohlone Sky?

A privately funded aerial gondola system connecting San Francisco’s Pier 24 to Yerba Buena and Treasure Island. It’s transit with a view — designed to inspire and built to last.

Why build a gondola?

Because nothing else solves this problem so efficiently. It’s fast, carbon-free, visually stunning, and doesn’t clog the roads or cost taxpayers billions.

Why here?

Treasure Island is undergoing a major revitalization, with up to 8,000 new homes planned. Ohlone Sky is designed to meet the island’s growing transit needs — offering a scenic, zero-emission alternative to driving. It’s not a replacement for existing systems, but a complement. Our goal is to expand mobility, not compete with it. Together, we can help transform Treasure Island into a thriving, well-connected neighborhood.

How many people will ride it?

We estimate at least two million roundtrip riders annually — including tourists, commuters, and Treasure Island residents.

Where is Pier 24?

It's across the street from the Hills Bros building on the Embacadero waterfront almost under the Bay Bridge. It's one-half mile (800 metres) or a moderate ten-minute walk south of the Ferry Building.

Is this just a tourist attraction?

No. It’s daily transportation — beautiful, efficient, and equitable. Tourists love it, but locals will rely on it.

Is it eco-friendly?

Yes. It’s powered by electricity, produces no tailpipe emissions.

Does it protect the Bay?

Absolutely. The system glides over water, avoiding marine disruption and reducing runoff pollution from car traffic.

What about the view?

We’ve partnered with leading architects to design towers and stations that respect and elevate the beauty of San Francisco’s skyline.

Who’s paying for this?

Private investors, philanthropic sponsors, and eventually ticket revenue — not San Francisco taxpayers.

How much will it cost?

As of spring 2025, the estimated project cost is $325 million. Capital funding will come primarily from bond financing, repaid through project revenue — mainly fares and sponsorships. All operations and maintenance costs will be covered entirely by fare revenue.


What does San Francisco contribute?

Only insurance coverage — no funding, no cost to the City. We’re not asking for public money. If bonds are used, the project repays them in full.

How long is the ride?

About twelve minutes from end to end — much faster than driving or taking a bus. The ferry's quicker although we're more frequent.

How often do cabins run?

Every 20-30 seconds. No long waits, no crowding, just step aboard.

Is it ADA accessible?

Yes. Cabins are wheelchair-friendly, stroller-friendly, and easy to board for all ages and abilities. BYO bike.

What happens in bad weather?

The system is designed to operate safely in wind, fog, and rain — weather San Franciscans know well. Operations may be suspended during high winds, but this is for passenger comfort, not safety. Our 3S gondolas are engineered to operate in winds up to 68 mph. Riders will stay dry, comfortable, and above the fray — even when the fog rolls in.

Who benefits from Ohlone Sky?

Everyone. Treasure Island residents, downtown workers, small businesses, and tourists.

Do you give back?

Ohlone Sky commits 10% of its annual revenue to charity — supporting local communities, climate initiatives, and cultural organizations. As a certified B Corp, we’re dedicated to using business as a force for good. We are transit with a conscience.

What about the Ohlone people?

We acknowledge the Ramaytush Ohlone people, who are the traditional custodians of this land. We pay our respects to the Ramaytush Ohlone elders — past, present, and future — who call this place, the lands that this project sits upon, their home. Ohlone Sky takes its name in recognition of that history — a gesture of respect, not replacement. Learn more about the Ramaytush Ohlone.

Is this legally approved?

It will be. We’re entering a 2–3 year environmental and permitting process with local, state, and federal agencies.

Will the public have input?

Yes. Community voices matter, and we’ll host public sessions during the design and approval phases.

How sustainable is Ohlone Sky?

We advance goals around clean energy, resilient infrastructure, equitable mobility, climate action, and community uplift — not just for San Francisco, but for the world we want to live in. We support those solving challenges aligned with the United Nations’ 17 Sustainable Development Goals.

What are some examples of gondolas and aerial trams?

Locally, Oakland has the super cool Oakland Zoo Gondola which sports a six-county bird's-eye view over live oak trees, bison, camels and elephants. San Francisco sports a little-used gondola to Salesforce Park (atop the Salesforce Transit Center) from Mission Street. In Napa, Sterling Vineyards lays claim to "the only winery in the world with a aerial gondola." In Oregon, the Portland Aerial Tram connects South Waterfront to Marquam Hill with 10,000 daily riders. In Colorado, the Telluride Village Gondola "offers free, seasonal transportation for the public." Some consider it the most beautiful commute in the United States. We'll concede for now. In New York, the Roosevelt Island Tramway connects the Upper East Side to Roosevelt Island.
