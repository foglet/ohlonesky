Why Ohlone Sky?

Ohlone Sky isn’t just a fast, electric connection across the Bay — it’s a blueprint for a more just, sustainable, and inspiring city. Clean, quiet, and architecturally stunning, it links Pier 24, Yerba Buena Island, and Treasure Island in just twelve minutes.

It reduces inequality with every trip

A twelve-minute ride gives Treasure Island families the same mobility as downtown residents — without tolls, traffic, or detours. Ohlone Sky closes the last-mile gap, replacing long commutes with fast, frequent, and affordable transit. That’s not just a convenience — it’s a statement about what kind of city we want to be.

It’s climate-friendly by design

Electric and low-emission, aerial gondolas reduce car trips, ease congestion, and lower greenhouse gases — especially critical as Treasure Island grows. With a minimal physical footprint and near-silent operation, Ohlone Sky is the sustainable transit option that enhances rather than disrupts the cityscape.

It’s resilient, scalable infrastructure

Gondola systems are modular: to increase capacity, just add cabins. They integrate easily with existing networks like Muni and BART. With headways as short as 20 seconds and peak capacity above 3,000 riders per hour per direction, Ohlone Sky adapts to demand without the need for new roads, ferries, or tunnels.

It powers inclusive economic growth

Ohlone Sky connects workers to jobs, tourists to small businesses, and investors to opportunity. It will energize Treasure Island’s new neighborhoods — many with below-market-rate homes — while expanding the city’s transit-accessible labor pool. The result: more equitable access to employment, stronger local economies, and increased tax revenues to fund public services.

It’s visually stunning and architecturally ambitious

Like the gondolas of Medellín, London, or New York, Ohlone Sky will become a new icon on the Bay — a floating thread that ties together our past, present, and future. With a world-class design team ready to create a structure worthy of the skyline, it offers donors and sponsors the rare chance to redefine the visual identity of San Francisco — forever.
