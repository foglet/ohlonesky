The need and desire for more transit on Treasure Island is well-documented. The San Francisco County Transportation Authority (SFCTA) states in its reports and guidelines: "Treasure Island is a unique San Francisco island neighborhood where residents, workers, and visitors have limited transportation options to access essential services, jobs, and leisure activities on the mainland." "Currently, the only public transportation option is the Muni 25 bus, which has only one stop in Downtown San Francisco at the Transbay Terminal. Planned transportation improvements to support development including the recently launched ferry service will be phased in over time, including new ferry service to and from the San Francisco ferry terminal, new AC Transit bus service to and from downtown Oakland BART stations, expanded Muni service, an on-island shuttle, and other supplemental services like carshare and bikeshare." p. 6 https://www.sfcta.org/sites/default/files/2023-09/SFCTA_CAC_Treasure%20Island%20Supplemental%20Transportation%20Study%20REPORT_2023-09-06.pdf >> p. 18 Similarly, their Treasure Island Transportation Program (SFCTA) sets transit guidelines on Treasure Island.
